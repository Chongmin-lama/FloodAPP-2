FLOODGUARD — What Does This App Do?
=====================================

FloodGuard is a platform that helps communities and authorities work together
during flood events. Anyone can stay informed, and registered users can report
floods and track responses in real time.


WHO USES THIS APP?
------------------

There are three types of people who use FloodGuard:

  CITIZENS
  Regular people living in flood-prone areas. They can report floods they
  witness and track what's happening in their community.

  AUTHORITIES
  Local emergency response officers or officials. They review reports,
  send out teams, and issue official warnings to the public.

  ADMINS
  Platform managers who oversee the system, manage user accounts,
  and keep everything running smoothly.


WHAT CAN THE PUBLIC DO? (No account needed)
--------------------------------------------

  - Visit the homepage and see a live feed of the latest flood alerts
    issued by authorities across Nepal.

  - Go to the "View Alerts" page to browse all official alerts, filter
    them by severity (critical, high, medium, low), and read the full
    details of each alert.

  - Open the "View Map" page to see an interactive map of Nepal with
    pins showing where flood alerts have been issued. Hovering over a
    pin shows the alert title, location, description, and severity.

  - No account or login is required for any of the above.


WHAT CAN CITIZENS DO?
----------------------

  - Create a free account in under a minute.

  - Submit a flood report with details like:
      * Which district and specific location is affected
      * How severe the flooding is (low / medium / high / critical)
      * The current water level (e.g. ankle level, above knee)
      * A description of the situation
      * A contact number for follow-up

  - View all the reports they have submitted and track their current
    status (pending, verified, teams responding, resolved, or rejected).

  - Edit or delete their own reports as long as they haven't been
    reviewed yet.


WHAT CAN AUTHORITIES DO?
-------------------------

  Everything a citizen can do, plus:

  - See ALL flood reports submitted by anyone across Nepal in one place.

  - Move reports through a response pipeline:
      * Mark a report as Verified after confirming it's real
      * Mark it as Responding once a team has been dispatched
      * Mark it as Resolved once the situation is handled
      * Reject reports that turn out to be false or duplicates

  - Edit or delete any report if needed.

  - Publish official flood alerts that appear on the public homepage,
    the View Alerts page, and the map. These alerts include a title,
    the affected district and area, severity level, and a message
    with guidance for the public.


WHAT CAN ADMINS DO?
--------------------

  Everything an authority can do, plus:

  - View a full list of all registered users on the platform.

  - Change a user's role — for example, promoting a citizen to an
    authority account when they join an emergency response team.

  - Reset a user's password if they are locked out of their account.

  - Remove a user from the platform entirely if needed.


SEVERITY LEVELS EXPLAINED
--------------------------

  LOW       Minor flooding, no immediate danger. Stay alert.
  MEDIUM    Moderate flooding. Avoid low-lying areas.
  HIGH      Serious flooding. Evacuation of at-risk areas advised.
  CRITICAL  Severe, life-threatening flooding. Immediate action required.


THE REPORT JOURNEY
------------------

  When a citizen submits a flood report, here is what happens:

    1. PENDING     — Report has been submitted and is waiting for review.
    2. VERIFIED    — An authority has confirmed the report is genuine.
    3. RESPONDING  — A response team has been dispatched to the location.
    4. RESOLVED    — The situation has been handled and is under control.

  At any point, an authority can also mark a report as REJECTED if it
  is found to be inaccurate or a duplicate.
